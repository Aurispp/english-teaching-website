# Local Partnership And Referral Source Map

Created: 2026-05-22 Cycle 22

Purpose: map local and professional communities around Castelldefels, Gava, Viladecans and Barcelona that could become warm referral sources later, without cold-spamming, joining irrelevant groups, or making Auris look like an academy.

## Primary Decision

Do not start cold outreach to local organisations yet.

The better next step is a relationship-source map: identify where Spanish-speaking adults, professionals, autonomos, small businesses and international workers already gather, then test the fit through observation, owner-side attendance, or warm introductions only if Auris chooses.

The highest-fit lane is:

`local professional confidence referrals`

Not:

`corporate training provider`, `academy partner`, `discount referral programme`, or `generic English conversation meetup`.

## Why This Matters

The referral/review loop from Cycle 21 works best when students can easily describe who Auris helps. Local partnership research answers the next question: where do similar people already gather?

For Auris, useful sources should have at least two of these:

- professionals, freelancers, autonomos, founders or small teams
- English needed for work, clients, presentations, travel, international colleagues or relocation
- trust-based community rather than anonymous marketplace browsing
- local link to Castelldefels, Gava, Viladecans, Baix Llobregat or Barcelona
- adults, not mainly children/exams
- a format where a personal teacher is credible

## Practical Strategy

### Best First Step

Start with relationship-led referrals from current and former students, not external organisations.

Reason:

- It has the highest trust transfer.
- It fits the personal-teacher brand.
- It needs no public promises, new pages or cold outreach.
- It can reveal whether the professional-confidence sentence is repeatable.

### Best Second Step

Build a local professional source list, but keep it read-only until there is a clearer offer.

The offer to test later:

`English confidence for Spanish-speaking professionals: meetings, presentations, interviews and client calls, with personal correction and between-class review.`

Spanish:

`Confianza en ingles para profesionales hispanohablantes: reuniones, presentaciones, entrevistas y llamadas con clientes, con correccion personal y repaso entre clases.`

### Best Third Step

If Auris wants to test local networking manually, choose one event/community where he would attend as a person, not as a salesperson.

Good fit:

- freelancers/autonomos
- small business owners
- tech/startup people
- international professionals based around Barcelona/Baix Llobregat
- communication/public-speaking events where English clarity is naturally relevant

Bad fit:

- generic language exchange where most people want free practice
- child/family academy fairs
- corporate procurement events that expect FUNDAE/large-provider framing
- mass cold-email lists

## One-Sentence Introductions

Use only in natural conversation, not as cold copy.

English:

`I help Spanish-speaking adults and professionals feel more confident using English in real situations, especially meetings, interviews, presentations and everyday conversation.`

Spanish:

`Ayudo a adultos y profesionales hispanohablantes a usar el ingles con mas confianza en situaciones reales, sobre todo reuniones, entrevistas, presentaciones y conversacion del dia a dia.`

Shorter referral version:

`If you know someone who knows English but freezes when they need to use it, that is exactly the kind of person I help.`

Spanish:

`Si conoces a alguien que sabe ingles pero se bloquea cuando tiene que usarlo, esa es justo la persona a la que suelo ayudar.`

## What Not To Do Yet

- Do not contact La Guaita, AEPEG, PIMEC, Cambra, coworking spaces or event organisers from automation.
- Do not pitch "English classes for your members" cold.
- Do not create a discount partnership offer.
- Do not create a new public partnership page.

- Do not attend generic language-exchange events as a lead strategy unless the goal is only research.

## Falsification

Idea we want to be true:

`Local professional communities can become a stronger referral source than cold social posting.`

What would weaken it:

- Events are mostly students/tourists/free-practice seekers.
- Communities are dominated by institutional providers.
- Auris does not want to attend/network in person.
- Leads from these spaces expect 10-20 EUR/h marketplace pricing.
- No one naturally describes a need around meetings, confidence, interviews or client calls.

Cheaper alternative that could beat it:

- Current-student referrals may outperform all external partnerships because trust is already present.

Decision:

- Keep local partnerships as a mapped expansion lane.
- Do not activate it before the review/referral micro-sprint or a clearer owner-side signal.
