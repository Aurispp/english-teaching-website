# Content Strategy

Last updated: 2026-05-24 Cycle 56

## Strategic Role

Content should prove how Auris teaches before someone books. It should make the viewer think: "This person understands exactly why I freeze in English, and he seems like someone I could actually speak with."

Do not chase generic English-teacher virality. Use content as a trust bridge from Google Business Profile, Instagram/TikTok/LinkedIn, Superprof/Tusclases, and the website.

## Audience

Primary:

- Spanish speakers in Spain, especially adults and professionals.
- People who have studied English for years but still freeze when speaking.
- Learners who want practical conversation, work English, Cambridge support, or confidence.
- Local Castelldefels/Gava/Viladecans people who prefer a personal teacher over an academy.

Secondary:

- Small teams, autonomos, and professionals who need invoice-capable English support.
- Parents of teens, where Auris's friendly but serious style and reviews matter.

## Message Pillars

1. Spanish-speaker blocks
   - Mental translation.
   - Spanish structures leaking into English.
   - Pronunciation traps.
   - False friends and collocations.
   - "I understand it, but I cannot say it."

2. Confidence through speaking
   - Real correction without embarrassment.
   - Speaking from minute one.
   - Better phrases for real situations.
   - The idea that confidence comes from repeated, guided use.

3. Professional English
   - Meetings, calls, interviews, presentations.
   - Saying things naturally and clearly.
   - Short role-play examples.
   - Before/after versions of clunky Spanish-influenced English.

4. Personal teacher, not academy
   - Direct relationship with Auris.
   - Classes adapted to one learner or small group.
   - Warm, practical, human.
   - No need to shout "freelance teacher"; show it through tone and specificity.

5. Platform as continuity
   - Vocabulary from class does not disappear.
   - Flashcards and review are personalised.
   - Materials and progress stay organised.
   - The tech exists to support speaking, not replace teaching.

6. Social proof
   - Review snippets.
   - Student progress stories without private details.
   - Common "after years of studying..." patterns.
   - First-class/free trial expectations.

## 30-Day Content Rhythm

Minimum viable cadence: 3 posts per week.

- 1 short teaching clip: one mistake, one better phrase, one quick practice prompt.
- 1 trust/proof post: review snippet, class moment, behind-the-scenes, platform detail.
- 1 professional/adult scenario: meeting phrase, interview answer, client-call wording, pronunciation correction.

Google Business Profile:

- 1 post per week.
- Add real class photos/platform screenshots where appropriate.
- Keep text local and practical.

LinkedIn:

- Reuse professional-English posts with a calmer, adult tone.
- Target professionals, small teams, and autonomos.

Instagram/TikTok:

- Use face-to-camera, whiteboard/screen, or class-tool clips.
- Keep posts tight: hook, correction, example, micro-practice.

## Cycle 49 Availability CTA Rule

The local website now has a capacity-aware availability direction ready for owner review/deployment: `Check availability`, `Ask about availability`, and the pricing note `I’m usually close to full, but you can message me about future availability.`

Content should follow the same capacity state:

| Capacity State | Public CTA | Content Tone | Measurement |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `full` / nearly full | Message me about future availability | Quiet proof, reviews, real class continuity, no urgency | Review replies, GBP profile freshness, future-demand notes |
| `limited` | Check availability | Honest, selective, warm; mention fit/timing | Inquiry timing, fit, source, availability confusion |
| `opening_soon` | Ask about the next available slot | One clear post/message, still personal | Day 0/Day 7 or Day 28 owner metrics plus inquiry notes |
| `actively_filling` | Ask about the next first class / first call | Directer CTA, still premium and personal | Contact clicks, direct replies, manually arranged trials, lead quality |

Do not combine "limited availability" with loud urgency or scarcity language. The goal is honest expectation-setting, not pressure.

## Talk the Talk Content Loop

Added: 2026-05-21 Cycle 04

Talk the Talk should give content a practical next step. Instead of every post ending in "book a class," some posts can invite the viewer to try one 60-90 second speaking prompt, then book if they want feedback.

### Weekly Pattern

Use one Talk the Talk prompt per week across GBP and social:

| Channel | Hook | Prompt Type | CTA | Measure |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Google Business Profile | Quick speaking practice for this week | Local/adult practical prompt | Try Talk the Talk | Website clicks, `talk_started` |
| Instagram/TikTok | If you freeze when speaking, try this | Everyday or work prompt | Try one free prompt | Profile clicks, `talk_completed` |
| LinkedIn | Professionals need safe repetitions of real situations | Meeting/interview/presentation prompt | Try the 90-second prompt | Link clicks, direct-contact clicks |

### Content Rules

- Keep it human and useful; do not make the tool sound like a separate app company.
- Lead with the speaking problem: freezing, translating, avoiding details, not knowing how to continue.
- Use the tool as a bridge to feedback: "Try it, notice where you got stuck, then we can fix that in class."
- Do not gate the tool behind email during the first test.
- Use UTM links for each channel so Talk starts/completions and `talk_contact_click` can be connected to source.

### First 6 Prompt Posts

| Theme | Hook | Prompt | CTA |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Confidence | If your mind goes blank in English, start smaller. | Describe your morning routine, but add one reason and one example. | Try a 90-second prompt. |
| Work | A meeting is not the place to discover your missing phrases. | Explain a problem at work and what you need from the other person. | Try Talk the Talk, then message me if you want feedback. |
| Interview | Practise before the question matters. | Tell me about a time you solved a difficult problem. | Try the prompt out loud. |
| Spanish-speaker block | Do you translate before speaking? Notice where it happens. | Talk about something you did yesterday and why it mattered. | Try one prompt and catch the pause. |
| Presentations | Clear English is built in small rehearsals. | Present one idea you think people should take seriously. | Try a 2-minute version. |
| Review proof | Students often say confidence grows when speaking feels normal. | Describe a conversation you avoided because of English. | Try the prompt, then bring it to class. |

### Measurement Window

Run this for 4 weeks. The test is not "did the post go viral?" The test is:

- Did people click into the tool?
- Did they start?
- Did they complete?
- Did any completers click the direct-contact CTA?
- Did current students mention using it between classes?

### Example LinkedIn Post

Hook:

`You understand English in meetings, but when someone asks for your opinion, your English suddenly becomes smaller.`

Body:

`That does not mean you "do not know English." It usually means your speaking is not automatic yet. You are translating, searching for the phrase, checking if the grammar is safe, and trying to sound professional at the same time.`

`A useful exercise: take one real work situation and explain it out loud for 90 seconds. Do not aim for perfect. Notice where you stop. That pause is the class material.`

CTA:

`I built Talk the Talk for short speaking prompts like this. Try one prompt, then bring the difficult part to class.`

### Example Short Reel

Hook:

`If you freeze in English, do this before your next meeting.`

Beat 1:

`Choose one situation: a problem, an update, or a question.`

Beat 2:

`Use these three phrases: "The issue is...", "What I need is...", "The next step is..."`

Beat 3:

`Say it out loud for 60 seconds. If you get stuck, perfect. That is what we fix.`

CTA:

`Try a free Talk the Talk prompt or book a trial if you want feedback.`
