# Professional English Job Demand Scan

Created: 2026-05-22 Cycle 23

Purpose: test whether the local/professional expansion lane is supported by current job-demand evidence, and translate that evidence into a practical offer/content plan for English with Auris.

## Primary Decision

The job-demand evidence strengthens the professional-confidence lane, but it does not justify cold corporate outreach yet.

The best offer shape is:

`Work-English Confidence Loop`

Not:

`generic Business English`, `corporate training provider`, `FUNDAE academy`, or `English for everyone at work`.

The demand is concrete and situational:

- speaking to clients and customers
- handling calls and emails
- explaining problems and updates
- setting up meetings
- preparing presentations and proposals
- negotiating or following up
- interviewing or moving into better roles
- working in international teams

## Strategic Interpretation

The strongest commercial truth is not "professionals need English."

It is:

`Professionals need English at the exact moment someone is listening: on a call, in a meeting, in an interview, in a presentation, or when a client has a problem.`

That is very compatible with Auris because his strongest proof already points to confidence, speaking comfort, adaptation, meetings, practical tools and continuity between classes.

The job evidence also refines the local partnership map:

- Coworking/startup and SME communities remain plausible because many roles involve clients, calls and proposals.
- Hospitality/MICE is a real Barcelona-specific context, but can be busy/price-sensitive and may prefer company training or internal training.
- Customer service/logistics/admin roles show B2-C1 demand, but may have lower wage/price tolerance than senior sales, tech or management roles.
- Sales, account management, customer success and project/client-facing tech roles look like better premium-fit contexts than generic "any worker who needs English."

## Falsification

Favored idea tested:

`Auris should build a professional-English offer around meetings, presentations, interviews and client calls.`

Result:

Strengthened, with a narrower definition.

What would weaken it:

- If leads mostly ask for certificate/exam-only English and do not care about real situations.
- If job-demand sources keep pointing to low-paid roles where 40 EUR/h is not viable.
- If owner-side referral leads do not mention work situations.
- If LinkedIn/GBP professional posts get views but no DMs, clicks or trial conversations.

Cheaper alternative that might beat it:

- Keep professional-English as a content/referral angle inside the normal private-class offer instead of building a separate product page or package.

## Offer Draft: Work-English Confidence Loop

Audience:

- Spanish-speaking adults and professionals who can usually express themselves but lose clarity or confidence in real work moments.
- Best fit: sales, account management, customer success, freelancers/autonomos, client-facing tech, hospitality/MICE, admin/customer support, job seekers preparing interviews.

Promise:

`Clearer, more confident English for the work situations where you cannot afford to freeze.`

What it includes:

- real conversation and role-play around the learner's work situations
- correction of Spanish-influenced patterns
- pronunciation and rhythm for clarity
- meeting/client-call phrases
- presentation/proposal practice
- interview answers and career story practice
- vocabulary and phrases saved in the student portal for review
- optional invoice with NIF

Suggested modules:

| Module | Use Case | Example Practice |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Meeting Voice | updates, opinions, clarification, disagreement | explain a problem and propose next steps |
| Client Calls | questions, incidents, follow-up, reassurance | handle a complaint or update a client |
| Presentations / Proposals | structure, transitions, Q&A | present one idea and answer two questions |
| Interviews / Career Moves | experience, strengths, examples | answer `tell me about a time...` naturally |
| Everyday Work English | emails, small talk, internal communication | rewrite direct Spanish translations into natural phrases |

## First Content Test From This Scan

Use one post, not a big campaign:

Hook:

`You do not need "business English" in general. You need English for the moment someone asks you a question in a meeting, interview or client call.`

Body:

`That is where many Spanish speakers lose confidence. They know the words, but they start translating, shorten the answer, avoid details, or sound less professional than they really are.`

`In class, I like to use real situations: explain a work problem, respond to a client, present an idea, answer an interview question. Then we fix the patterns that block you and save useful phrases for review between classes.`

CTA:

`If this sounds familiar, try a short Talk the Talk prompt or message me about a free first class.`

Best destination:

- LinkedIn: Talk the Talk UTM from the existing profile plan.
- GBP: homepage UTM until the business page has stronger proof/indexation.
