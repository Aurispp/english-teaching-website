## LinkedIn Drafts

Tone: calm, practical, adult. Write as Auris, not as an academy.

### LinkedIn 1: The Meeting Answer Gets Smaller

Hook:

`You understand the meeting, but when someone asks for your opinion, your English gets smaller.`

Body:

`This happens to a lot of Spanish-speaking professionals I work with.`

`It does not always mean your level is low. Very often it means you are doing four jobs at the same time: translating, checking grammar, searching for the perfect word, and trying to sound professional.`

`A useful exercise before a meeting: choose one real topic and answer out loud for 90 seconds. Do not aim for perfect English. Listen for the moment where you stop, simplify too much, or avoid the detail.`

`That pause is not a failure. It is class material.`

CTA:

`I made Talk the Talk for short speaking prompts like this. Try one prompt, then bring the difficult part to class if you want personal feedback.`

Link: Talk URL UTM `meeting_answer_smaller`

### LinkedIn 2: AI Practice And Human Feedback

Hook:

`AI can help you practise English more often. A teacher helps you understand what to fix.`

Body:

`I do not think those two things should fight each other.`

`For many learners, the biggest problem is not access to more content. It is knowing what is happening when they speak: where Spanish structure appears, which phrases sound unnatural, where pronunciation affects clarity, and why their answer becomes smaller under pressure.`

`Daily practice is useful. Personal diagnosis is what turns that practice into progress.`

CTA:

`Try a short speaking prompt, notice where you get stuck, and use that as the starting point for feedback.`

Link: Talk URL UTM `ai_practice_human_feedback`

### LinkedIn 3: Business English Is Too Generic

Hook:

`"Business English" is often too broad to be useful.`

Body:

`For one person it means client calls. For another, interviews. For another, presentations, writing clearer emails, or speaking up in meetings without sounding unsure.`

`That is why I prefer to start with the real situation, not the label. What do you need to say? Who are you speaking to? What happens when you hesitate? Which phrases are missing?`

`In a personal class or small group, those details matter.`

CTA:

`If your English goal is connected to work, start by writing down three situations where English costs you confidence. That is already a useful diagnosis.`

Link: professional page UTM `professional_confidence_loop`

### LinkedIn 4: Personal Teacher, Organised Follow-Up

Hook:

`The advantage of a personal English teacher should not be "less structure."`

Body:

`It should be the opposite: more relevant structure.`

`You get the direct relationship, the class adapted to your speaking habits, and the freedom to work on the exact situations that matter to you. But you should also have follow-up: vocabulary, class notes, review, small practice tasks and a clear way to continue between classes.`

`That is the system I keep building around my classes. Practical speaking with a human teacher, supported by organised review.`

CTA:

`For adults and professionals who want English to feel usable, not just studied.`

Link: homepage UTM `portal_continuity`

## Decision Rules After Two Weeks

Continue GBP + LinkedIn if:

- At least one qualified inquiry mentions a post/profile/review.
- GBP shows website clicks, calls or messages above baseline.
- LinkedIn creates profile views, DMs, link clicks or warm conversations with professionals.
- Talk the Talk receives source-tagged starts/completions/trial clicks from these links.

Shift emphasis toward GBP/reviews/local SEO if:

- GBP creates visible actions but LinkedIn creates none.
- Local/professional search queries increase but social links do not create qualified action.
- Leads mention Google/reviews rather than posts.

Use short-form only as reuse if:

- It gets views/saves but no profile clicks, messages, Talk starts, or qualified leads.
- Audience comments are broad learner attention rather than local/professional buying intent.

Stop or change the sprint if:

- The only responses are low-price marketplace-style inquiries.
- Posts create no visible action after two full cycles and the ledger is complete.
- Auris dislikes the posting rhythm enough that consistency is unrealistic.

## What Not To Do Yet
